Schema.org & Structured Data

Enhance your website's search engine visibility

What is Structured Data?

Structured data is a standardized format for providing information about a page and classifying the page content. Search engines use this data to better understand your content and create rich results.

JSON-LD Implementation

JSON-LD (JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data) is the recommended format for structured data. Here's a basic example:

{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "Article", "headline": "Your Article Title", "author": { "@type": "Person", "name": "Author Name" }, "datePublished": "2024-01-01", "image": "article-image.jpg" }

Benefits of Structured Data

Rich Snippets

Display enhanced search results with ratings, images, and more

Better CTR

Improve click-through rates with visually appealing results

Voice Search

Optimize for voice assistants and smart devices

Knowledge Graph

Increase chances of appearing in Google's Knowledge Graph

Common Schema Types

  • Article - News articles and blog posts
  • Product - E-commerce items with pricing
  • Recipe - Cooking instructions and ingredients
  • Event - Dates, locations, and tickets
  • Organization - Company information
  • Person - Individual profiles

Validation Tools

Always validate your structured data using Google's Rich Results Test to ensure it's properly formatted and will be recognized by search engines.
